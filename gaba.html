GABA

The primary inhibitory neurotransmitter of the central nervous system.

Overview

Gamma-aminobutyric Acid (GABA) is the main inhibitory neurotransmitter in the brain. It plays a critical role in reducing neuronal excitability and maintaining balance within neural circuits.

Synthesis

GABA is synthesized from glutamate through the enzyme glutamic acid decarboxylase (GAD). This conversion is essential for maintaining excitatory-inhibitory balance in the brain.

Receptors

GABA acts primarily on two receptor types:

  • GABA-A receptors - Ionotropic, fast inhibitory action via chloride channels.
  • GABA-B receptors - Metabotropic, slower modulatory inhibitory effects.

    • Functions

    • Regulation of anxiety and stress
    • Sleep modulation
    • control of motor activity
    • Prevention of excessive neuronal firing

    Clinical Relevance

    • Epilepsy
    • Anxiety disorders
    • Insomnia
    • Huntington's disease
