Acetylcholine

A neurotransmitter involved in memory, attention and muscle activation.

Overview

Acetylcholine (ACh) is one of the first neurotransmitter ever discovered. It plays a critical role in both the central nervous system (CNS) and peripheral nervous system (PNS), particulary in muscle contraction and cognitive function.

Synthesis

Acetylcholine is synthesized from choline and acetyl-CoA by the enzyme choline acetyltransferase. It is stored in synaptic vesicles and released into the synaptic cleft upon neuronal activation.

Receptors

Acetylcholine acts on two major reseptor types:

  • Memory formation
  • Attention and learning
  • Muscle contraction
  • Autonomic nervous system regulation

Clinical Relevance

  • Alzheimer's disease
  • Myastheinia gravis
  • Parkinson's disease
  • Neuromuscular disorders
