Serotonin

A monoamine neurotransmitter involved in mood, emotion, and sleep regulation.

Overview

Serotonin (5-hydroxytryptamine or 5-HT) is a monoamine neurotransmitter that plays a major role in regulating mood, anxiety, sleep, appetite, and emotional processing.

Receptors

Serotonin acts on multiple receptor families (5-HT1 to 5-HT7), most of which are metabotropic receptors, except 5HT3, which is ionotropic.

Functions

  • Mood regulation
  • Sleep-wake cycle control
  • Appetite regulation
  • Emotional stability
  • Pain percetion modulation

Clinical Relevance

  • Depression
  • Anxiety disorders
  • Obsessive-compulsive disorder (OCD)
  • Migraine
