Prefrontal cortex

The executive control center of the brain.

What is the PFC?

The prefrontal cortex (PFC) is the front-most part of the frontal lobe. It plays a crucial role in decision-making, impulse control, emotional regulation, planning, and goal-directed behavior.

Core Functions

  • Executive function & planning
  • Working memory
  • Impulse inhibition
  • Long-term strategy thinking
  • Personality expression

Why It Matters

A well-regulated PFC allows us to think before we act. When it's underdeveloped or overwhelmed by stress, emotional systems like the amygdala can dominate.
