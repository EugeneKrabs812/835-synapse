Histamine

What is Histamine

Histamine is a neurotransmitter and immune signaling molecule involved in wakefulness, alertness, and allergic responses. While many people associate histamine with allergies, it also plays important role in the brain.

In the central nervous system, histamine helps regulate sleep-wake cycles, attention, and appetite.

Main Functions

  • regulate wakefulness

    • Histamine promotes alertness and helps keep the brain awake. High histamine activity is associated with increased arousal and attention.

  • Controls sleep-wake cycle

    • It plays a key role in maintaining wakefulness. This is why antihistamine medications often cause drownsiness.

  • Involved in allergic responses

    • Outside the brain, histamine is released during allergic reactions, causing symptoms like itching, swelling, and inflammation.

  • Influences appetite

    • Histamine activity in the brain can help regulate hunger and energy balance.

  • Supports cognitive function

    • It contributes to attention and information processing.

If Levels Are Too Low

  • Allergic symptoms

    • Sneezing, itching, swelling, and inflammation can occur due to immune histamine release

  • Sleep disturbances

    • Overactivation may interfere with normal sleep regulation.

Interesting Facts

Many allergy medications work by blocking histamine receptors.

Antihistamines often cause drownsiness because they reduce brain histamine activity

Histamine-producing neurons are located in the hypothalamus
