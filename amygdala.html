Amygdala

The brain's emotional alarm system.

What is the Amygdala?

The amygdala is part of the limbic system and plays a central role in processing emotions, especially fear and threat detection.

Main Functions

  • Fear response
  • Emotional memory
  • Threat assesment
  • Fight-or-flight activation

PFC vs Amygdala

When the amygdala reacts quickly to danger, it can override the prefrontal cortex. This is why we sometimes react emotionally before thinking logically.
