Norepinephrine (Noradrenaline)

Norepinephrine often called the "chemical of alertness" because its role in improving focus and preparing the body for action.

Overview

Norepinephrine is both a neurotransmitter and hormine that plays a major role in the sympathetic nervous system. It helps the body respond to stress and increases alertness.

Main Functions

  • Increases focus and concentration
  • Regulates blood pressure
  • Raises heart rate
  • Activates the fight-or-flight response
  • Helps the body respond to stress

If Levels Are Too Low

  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Low energy
  • Depressive symptoms
  • Lack of motivation

If Levels Are Too High

  • Exercessive anxiety
  • Rapid heartbeat
  • High blood pressure
  • Trouble sleeping

    • Interesting facts

    • Norepinepherine is released during stressful or dangerous situations.
    • It works closely with adrenaline in the stress response.
    • Many ADHD and depression medication affect norepinephrine levels.
