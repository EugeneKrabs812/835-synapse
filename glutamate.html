Glutamate

The primary excitatory neurotransmitter of the central nervous system.

Overview

Glutamate is the principal excitatory neurotransmitter in the brain. It plays a fundamental role in synaptic transmission, neural plasticity, learning, memory, and overall cortical activation.

Synthesis

Glutamate is synthesized primarily from glutamine via the enzyme glutaminase within presynaptic neurons. It can also be derived from glucose metabolism through the Krebs cycle.

Receptors

Glutamate binds to ionotropic receptors (AMPA, NMDA, Kainate) and metabotropic receptors (mGluRs), which regulate synaptic transmission and plasticity.

Functions

  • Learning and memory formation
  • Long-term potentiation (LTP)
  • Synaptic plasticity
  • Excitation of neural circuits

Clinical Relevance

  • Epilepsy
  • Alzheimer’s disease
  • Schizophrenia
  • Stroke-related neuronal damage
